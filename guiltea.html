Overview

Context

  • Type: Website Redesign
  • Duration: 10 weeks | 2018 (ongoing)
  • Team: 4 members | cross-functional
  • Client: Hue (manager), Guiltea Cravings
Guiltea Cravings is an independently-owned boba and dessert cafe located in sunny eastern San Diego that offers a multitude of tasty drinks and snacks. The store specializes in variety of tea, smoothies, coffee, macarons, ice cream, and savory snacks. A stand-out characteristic, compared to other boba shops in this boba-populated part of San Diego, is that drinks can be customized to each customer’s unique preferences and menu items.


Problem

☁ How might we redesign a boba website?

The website needed to be reorganized for easier navigation and straightforward use. The site does not receive much traffic, so we aimed to increase customer engagement with the site through interactive content and helpful information. We wanted to emphasize the uniqueness of Guiltea Cravings by highlighting what they offer and what sets them apart from the competitors. By revamping the site, we hoped the company’s brand shines through and can grow to be one that customers know and trust. In order to express their originality as a stand-out boba shop, we will redesign their current website to convey a modern yet friendly aesthetic through thoughtful and informative contents to cater to the boba enthusiasts of San Diego.

1st Phase

☉ Strategize: Client Interview

After the client agreed to work with us for this quarter-long project, we first met up with him to get an idea of his business and learn what content and features he wants for a redesign of the website. We learned in-depth about how the company grew from client's passion and love towards baking and cooking, so it was important to portray Guiltea’s distinctiveness by accentuating bright colors and attention-grabbing visuals. We were able to discover the main themes of Guiltea Cravings, what they serve, and how they differentiate from other boba places. Then, we proceeded to conduct a website needfinding with the client, identifying features he wants to keep or remove as we navigated through the current site. We aimed to improve on the interactive content of the site while keeping the clicks minimal so that it remains easy for the user to navigate through the site. We were then able to set priorities primarily based on clients' needs and interests.

❥ Empathize: User Research

We conducted user interviews at Guiltea Cravings at various times and days of the week. We also conducted some interviews offsite in-person or through phone. Our participants varied from students to professionals to parents. Our interviews helped us gain an understanding of customers’ experiences with Guiltea Cravings, competing boba shops, and the current website.

    Example Interview Questions
  1. What do you think of boba? How often do you drink boba?
  2. When/what time of day do you go to get boba?
  3. What are some characteristics you look for in boba shops?
  4. How do you choose which boba shop you want to go to that day?
  5. Who do you go to boba shops with and what are some things you do when you get there?
  6. What are some items you like at boba shops (milk tea, coffee, snacks, desserts, etc.)? How do you go about choosing those items?
  7. How did you find out about Guiltea Cravings?
  8. Have you ever visited the Guiltea website?
  9. Why do you visit restaurant/cafe websites?
  10. Do you use any rewards program? Why or why not?

Based on our findings through interviews, we came up with 3 user personas:
  • ‣ Boba Expert: Frequent boba-drinkers who knows various drink types. We define ‘expert’ as someone who has been to more than 5 different boba shops and tried more than 5 different boba drinks.
  • ‣ Boba Novice: New to boba drinks and are not familiar with the various drinks. We define 'novice' as a new customer who has not been to very many boba shops and not tried more than 5 different boba drinks.
  • ‣ Potential Customer: Been to boba shops before, and are potential customers at Guiltea Cravings. We define ‘potential customer’ as a user who would find Guiltea fitting their specific needs in terms of drinks, programs, and service.
We also based and created our user scenarios and use cases on these interviews. Thus, the frequency in personas reflect the data gained from the interviews as it was evident that many interviewees were curious especially about the menu and its photos. And we will later see how high-quality photos were something we dealt with crucially during the design process.



2nd Phase

✐ Analyze: Competitve Analysis & Moodboard

We identified our four main competitiors, Kung Fu Tea, Square Bar, Tastea, and Boba Guys to specify takeaways and disadvantages amongst the competitors' sites. We focused on analyzing each site's brand (cohesiveness, uniqueness, and prominence), functionality (interactions/features), content, site architecture, design, and other good ideas we might consider using.


    Strengths
  • ✔︎ Consistent, colorful fonts and images with a clean look (Tastea)
  • ✔︎ Interactive, engaging features throughout the site (Boba Guys)
  • ✔︎ Clear representation of rewards and fundraising info (Tastea)
  • ✔︎ Showcase pictures of the shops, their products, and customers (all)
  • ✔︎ Emphasis on store's background story and products (Boba Guys)
    Weaknesses
  • ✘ Basic and boring layout with generic designs (Square Bar)
  • ✘ Certain features only available on desktop (Kung Fu)
  • ✘ Small, hard-to-read fonts and long scroll on mobile(Boba Guys)
  • ✘ Confusing categorization amongst menu, products, and merchandise (Kung Fu)
  • ✘ Poor transition from web to mobile losing some design aspects (Boba Guys)

From in-depth analysis, we now intended to not only effectively express brand's overall vibe and style but also highligh important information and calls-to-action (i.e. Our Story, reward program) while avoiding cognitive overload for users. We aimed to maintain a clean, minimal look with splashes of bright colors, and give users easy access to information by minimizing scrolling and excess visuals.



❂ Design: Wireframing

After shaping the general scope for our project design through our Creative Brief, we identified our three target audience for Guiltea Cravings: boba enthusiasts, college students, and boba novice or first-time customers. All these customers will benefit from the friendly, welcoming vibe that the site gives off, along with the clear navigation and contents it provides. The goal of this website is to allow users to feel the welcoming ambience of the business and its brand, as if they’re visiting store through the site. We will aim this by approaching a friendly and convenient user experience, providing them with easy-to-use functions, colorful and engaging images, and straightforward contents. After organizing description of major categories, we generated a site architecture diagram portraying the navigation bar for our prototype.


Low Res Mobile Wireframe


The overall personality and tone that Guiltea Cravings will express through the website will revolve around being friendly, inviting, and fun. We believe that these characteristics will help attract customers to the quaint and wholesome boba shop. We will maintain a minimal yet vibrant interface with some eye-catching graphics and imagery. The tone of the writing style expressed throughout the website will be chatty and personal in order to create an engaging method of communication with customers. Upon visiting the website, we want to ensure that the users are not overloaded with too much content. This requires a balance between informative text and meaningful visuals. The color palette of the website will consist of a blend of bright colors, and their more muted hues. The brighter colors will be used to emphasize call-to-action buttons and interactions. The muted colors can act as the foundation for the website. The imagery we incorporate into the site will consist of candid images to emphasize who the customers are and what they enjoy at Guiltea Cravings. From the competitor sites, we saw that editing imagery to follow a cohesive style guide was eye-catching. As a result, we aim to emulate this methodology in bringing attention to the meaningful images and content we add. VI. Key Target Audience Insight

3rd Phase

❀ Develop: Iteration & Concept Selection

After testing 3 different prototypes to users, we found that the integrated widget was the most balanced concept and had them most to offer our client. We leaned more towards the Eventbrite widget, since it has a secure, flexible, and large support service and our client expressed his approval for it. Another advantage of choosing the widget as our selected concept was that we can incorporate elements of the ground-up HTML/CSS website into the solution while taking advantage of a trusted service. Though creating it from ground-up would be ideal as it would come as no cost to both our client and the ticket buyers, we concluded that it would not be very feasible due to time and other constraints. After we receieved access to the code for the widget once we selected our layout, we redesigned the user interface of the client’s website to incorporate all required information with brand consistency and the widget at the heart of the page. The webpage also contains an interactive map, showing the location of the concert and the Facebook Page for the event.

❐ Evaluate: Usability Testing

Now that our prototype is in its final stage, we decided to test it out with our client John and the same people we initially interviewed. We had them go through the process of locating the event and purchasing a combination of lawn, table seats, and VIP table tickets. For testing purposes, we made all the tickets free of cost. Due to the request of our client, we decided to not let our buyers select their own seating arrangements, so we were unable to show the user where they will be sitting during the actual event. Thus, we planned to create a seating chart for the venue that corresponds to the seating assignment on each ticket to remedy this problem.

    Purchase Procedure
  1. User selects the desired ticket type and quantity on the widget and clicks the “Order Now” button.
  2. User is redirected to Eventbrite checkout page where they can view the total cost and enter billing and ticket holder information.
  3. Once the order is placed, the user receives the tickets immediately by email and can either print out tickets or use the Eventbrite app to display their tickets at the venue.


Even though there was minimal testing done on mobile, we went through the process ourselves to work on resolving issues such as difficulty scrolling through ticket options without accidentally scrolling down the website. Since mobile devices are a convenient way for users to show their tickets at the door, we recommended users and the client to utlilize the Eventbrite app to pull up their tickets (QR code) on the phone to scan. Besides the minor issues with the mobile aspect, the main concern that emerged from testing is with the VIP tables. Many users including our client, found it confusing that the only choices for this option were 0 OR 10 tickets. We intended to define 10 as 10 seats at the table, but one of the users mentioned that it would make more sense to count it as 1 ticket for 1 table.

Through many testing and iterations, we found out not only is the VIP table option confusing, but also that during checkout, each ticket asks for a name, email, and mailing address. Users had to tediously fill out this information 10 times under eight minutes, so we kept these concerns in mind as we further refine our prototype.

Conclusion

❈ Aftermath

To address the concerns about the VIP Table option that arose from user testing, there is now a “More Info” button next to each of the ticket types, providing a brief description about the differences in ticket seating. We decided to leave 0 or 10 option as-is because we want each attendee to have a ticket for easy access into the event. Additionally, we removed the extra, time-consuming process of inputting user's home address when purchasing group tickets. Now, there is the option of copying the data from another ticket and the email address of one person.

With our design finalized, our client may choose to either incorporate the widget into their own website, or take inspiration from our design and move onto a different ticketing service. If our client chooses to use our design, he can view our product manual to make any necessary changes for future events, such as event information and seating arrangement map. Overall, our solution appropriately completes our client’s requirements while still being feasible and affordable. Accordingly, our research consisted of personally finding and testing ticketing services and conducting user testing on selected ticketing services. This research and development paved the way for our final design, a fully functional web page with a built-in eventbrite widget.
