Guiltea Cravings

Digitally conquering your dessert cravings

FINAL PROTOTYPE

Overview

Context

Type: Website Redesign

Duration: 3 months | 2018

Team: 4 members | cross-functional

Client: Hue (manager)

Guiltea Cravings is an independently-owned boba and dessert cafe located in eastern San Diego that offers a multitude of tasty drinks and snacks. The store specializes in variety of tea, smoothies, coffee, macarons, ice cream, and savory snacks. A stand-out characteristic, compared to other places in this boba-populated part of San Diego, is that drinks can be customized to customer’s preferences.


Problem

How might we redesign a boba website to portray the store's unique brand and specializations?

The website needed to be reorganized for easier navigation and straightforward use. The site does not receive much traffic, so we aimed to increase customer engagement with the site through interactive content and helpful information. We wanted to emphasize the uniqueness of Guiltea Cravings by highlighting what they offer and what sets them apart from the competitors. By revamping the site, we hoped the company’s brand shines through and can grow to be one that customers know and trust. In order to express their originality as a stand-out boba shop, we will redesign their current website to convey a modern yet friendly aesthetic through thoughtful and informative contents to cater to the boba enthusiasts of San Diego.

    1st phase

    Client Interview

    After the client agreed to work with us for this quarter-long project, we first met up with him to get an idea of his business and learn what content and features he wants for a redesign of the website. We learned in-depth about how the company grew from client's passion and love towards baking and cooking, so it was important to portray Guiltea’s distinctiveness by accentuating bright colors and attention-grabbing visuals. We were able to discover the main themes of Guiltea Cravings, what they serve, and how they differentiate from other boba places. Then, we proceeded to conduct a website needfinding with the client, identifying features he wants to keep or remove as we navigated through the current site. We aimed to improve on the interactive content of the site while keeping the clicks minimal so that it remains easy for the user to navigate through the site. We were then able to set priorities primarily based on clients' needs and interests.

    User Research

    We conducted user interviews at Guiltea Cravings at various times and days of the week. We also conducted some interviews offsite in-person or through phone. Our participants varied from students to professionals to parents. Our interviews helped us gain an understanding of customers’ experiences with Guiltea Cravings, competing boba shops, and the current website.

    1. What do you think of boba? How often do you drink boba?
    2. When/what time of day do you go to get boba?
    3. What are some characteristics you look for in boba shops?
    4. How do you choose which boba shop you want to go to that day?
    5. Who do you go to boba shops with and what are some things you do when you get there?
    6. What are some items you like at boba shops? How do you go about choosing those items?
    7. How did you find out about Guiltea Cravings?
    8. Have you ever visited the Guiltea website?
    9. Why do you visit restaurant/cafe websites?
    10. Do you use any rewards program? Why or why not?

    Based on our findings through interviews, we came up with 3 user personas:
    • Boba Expert: Frequent boba-drinkers who knows various drink types. We define ‘expert’ as someone who has been to more than 5 different boba shops and tried more than 5 different boba drinks.
    • Boba Novice: New to boba drinks and are not familiar with the various drinks. We define 'novice' as a new customer who has not been to very many boba shops and not tried more than 5 different boba drinks.
    • Potential Customer: Been to boba shops before, and are potential customers at Guiltea Cravings. We define ‘potential customer’ as a user who would find Guiltea fitting their specific needs in terms of drinks, programs, and service.

    We also based and created our user scenarios and use cases on these interviews. Thus, the frequency in personas reflect the data gained from the interviews as it was evident that many interviewees were curious especially about the menu and its photos. And we will later see how high-quality photos were something we dealt with crucially during the design process.

    2nd phase

    Competitive Analysis & Moodboard

    We identified our four main competitiors – Kung Fu Tea, Square Bar, Tastea, and Boba Guys – to specify strengths and weaknesses amongst the competitors' sites. We focused on analyzing each site's brand (cohesiveness, uniqueness, and prominence), functionality (interactions/features), content, site architecture, design, and other good ideas we might consider using.

    STRENGTHS

    Consistent, colorful fonts and images with a clean look

    Interactive, engaging features throughout the site

    Clear representation of rewards and fundraising info

    Showcase pictures of the shops, their products, and customers

    Emphasis on store's background story and products

    WEAKNESSES

    Basic and boring layout with generic designs

    Certain features only available on desktop

    Small, hard-to-read fonts and long scroll on mobile

    Confusing categorization amongst menu, products, and merchandise

    Poor transition from web to mobile

    From in-depth analysis, we now intended to not only effectively express brand's overall vibe and style but also highligh important information and calls-to-action (i.e. Our Story, reward program) while avoiding cognitive overload for users. We aimed to maintain a clean, minimal look with splashes of bright colors, and give users easy access to information by minimizing scrolling and excess visuals.

    Lo-Fi Wireframing

    After shaping the general scope for our project design through our Creative Brief, we identified our three target audience for Guiltea Cravings: boba enthusiasts, college students, and boba novice or first-time customers. All these customers will benefit from the friendly, welcoming vibe that the site gives off, along with the clear navigation and contents it provides. The goal of this website is to allow users to feel the welcoming ambience of the business and its brand, as if they’re visiting store through the site. We will aim this by approaching a friendly and convenient user experience, providing them with easy-to-use functions, colorful and engaging images, and straightforward contents. After organizing description of major categories, we generated a site architecture diagram portraying the navigation bar for our prototype.


    Low Res Mobile Wireframe


    The overall personality and tone that Guiltea Cravings will express through the website will revolve around being friendly, inviting, and fun. We worked to implement these themes into our Low-Fidelity Prototypes by aiming to portray minimal yet vibrant interface with gradients and eye-catching imagery. Upon visiting the website, we want to ensure that the users are not overloaded with too much content. This requires a balance between informative text and meaningful visuals, as noted in the functional specification explanations below. Brighter colors will be used to emphasize call-to-action buttons and interactions and muted colors can act as the foundation for the website. As a result, we aim to emulate this methodology in bringing attention to the significant images, content, and functions we add.

    3rd phase

    Hi-Fi Prototyping & Iteration


    Our goal of this prototype was to have enough of the look and feel to motivate our client and to collect feedback to make the necessary modifications to our final product. In this stage, I was responsible for typing up necessary descriptions and re-coding the menu page from our bootstrapped theme (e.g. gather images for menu ites, divide categories into sections, hover-over descriptions, Grubhub button). A general layout was implemented, but we had to gain feedback and adjust according to our client's needs. Finding high-quality banner pictures required more time and effort, so we deployed temporary placeholder banners for our first prototype.

    Iterative Testing

    Since conducting usability testing for Prototype 1 with ourselves and various users for minor edits and mobile-to-desktop optimization, we made another appointment with our client to acquire necessary evaluations and comments.

    Client Feedback
    • Add questions (or link) for "Join Our Team"
    • Liked the hover-descriptions function
    • Revise homepage to be more attention-grabbing
    • Include photos of customers enjoying drinks/food
    • Revise signature items (one from each category)
    • Happier and brighter photos
    • Color scheme and gradient is nice and subtle
    • Try to create a Site ID (catchphrase)


    We continued to finalize elements such as fonts and make sure that our design is consistent throughout the site. Some of the challenges we faced throughout this process revolved around really understanding the brand that the client wanted. Finding good quality images was really difficult because the images he provided were often too small. We attempted to take pictures ourselves and digged through hundreds of photos on store's Yelp page, but overall getting the right imagery was a challenge as well as making the images responsive because the sizes were all different. We revamped the whole menu page from our 1st prototyped because one of the core objective was to showcase the menu in an effective and user-friendly way. The client stated how menu was outdated and it was constantly changing. So, we displayed a fixed list of signature items and provided access to list of current items and their prices.

    FINAL PROTOTYPE


    Overall, we were able to overcome challenges by thoroughly communicating with the client and conducting a lot of user testing to ensure that what we were doing was in fact appealing to users. After various check-ins with our client throughout the quarter, we were able to attain our goals of re-designing the site to look more modern and also minimize the number of clicks it takes to get to parts of the site.

    conclusion

    aftermath

    After the quarter was over, our client expressed his gratitude and interest in hosting our re-designed site for the business. We met up with the client once again in May to briefly go over minor changes that he wanted to suggest, and we agreed to set up files to work on GoDaddy (web hosting service the client uses for his current site) after we make those edits. We plan to have our website published live on client's domain by end of the school year in June.
