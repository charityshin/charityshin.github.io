CONCERT AT THE LAKES

Project Details


VIEW PROTOTYPE

Overview

Context

  • Type: Web & System Redesign
  • Duration: 10 weeks | 2017
  • Team: 7 members | cross-functional
  • Client: Santee Lakeside Rotary Club
Our team worked with a nonprofit organization that gives back to the community in various ways (e.g aiding orphans, providing career counseling, sponsoring international students), and it's specifically known for holding an annual event, Concert at the Lakes. The goal of this event is to raise the necessary funds to provide incentives for high school students to graduate, and this incentive is a two-year scholarship to a college or to a business or trade school of their choice.


Problem

How might we create a better ticketing process for a renowned annual event?

Concert at the Lakes is such a renowned event amongst local residents in Santee City that tickets are sold out every year, so having a good ticketing system was essential. The previous event site redirected users to TicketRiver, where they can purchase a ticket for either a seat at a reserved table or an unreserved seating in an open lawn area. To add on, if users wanted to purchase a whole Table (for a group of people), they would have to go back to the original site and scroll down to fill out a different form. Thus, managers of Concert at the Lakes needed a better ticketing system because of current complications with seating arrangements and group ticket distributions. Our goal was to provide better user experience for ticket-buyers and better management system for site managers from Rotary Club.

1st Phase

☉ Strategize: Goals & Stakeholders

By interviewing the client in-person, we were able to understand and define our goals. In an attempt to fully meet our client's needs, our goal was to offer our client a ticketing sysytem that will be effectively integrated into their website. After prioritizing and setting an outline of our project approach, we determined our stakeholders by their levels of power and interest within the project venture. Then, we were able to create a brief affinity diagram of three primary concerns that addresses the current issues by our client.


❥ Empathize: User Research

We proceeded to conduct user research to gain insight on how potential concert-goers navigate through ticketing websites. In addition to in-person interviews, a ticketing system survey was distributed to collect more data from a greater range of people. We were able to determine people's preference of not only payment option but also ticketing sites from the online survey results.

    Interview Questions
  1. What is your preferred online payment method?
  2. Do you purchase things online? Regularly?
  3. Have you purchased tickets online? If so, what ticketing system do you normally use/prefer?
  4. What tickets do you usually buy? (e.g. Single, Table, Lawn)
  5. On average, how many tickets do you buy when attending an event? 
  6. How do you distribute these group tickets? 
  7. Have you ever had a bad experience purchasing tickets? If so, what was bad about this experience?
  8. How do you prefer to use your ticket? (i.e. physical or mobile ticket)
  9. When purchasing a ticket, would you be likely to join an email subscription?

Bar graph result from Google Forms


Then, based on our findings, we created an empathy map of what users may say, think, do, and feel as they navigate through the ticketing website. This was specifically designed for an event attendee and portrays the difficulties and shortcomings of its previous design.




2nd Phase

✐ Analyze: Personas

Through user research, we were able to learn more about the users and their areas of needs. We found at least three different kinds of user groups interacting with our design: middle-aged users who most likely buy tickets in major groups, the older retired users who may be more unfamiliar with the online ticket purchasing process, and managers who are in charge of handling the ticket system itself, updating the content and making sure things run smoothly.


Also, by analyzing AMC Ticketing Website, Valley View Casino Center Ticket Sales, and Amtrack Ticket Sales in depth, we were able to pinpoint some major pros (insights) and cons (what to avoid) from various competitive ticketing sites.

    Strengths
  • ✔︎ Modern, diagram-driven interface
  • ✔︎ Easy-to-navigate seat selection process
  • ✔︎ Use of electronic tickets
  • ✔︎ Flexibility of delivery methods and seat suggestions
  • ✔︎ Maintaining an email database for announcements
    Weaknesses
  • ✘ High money & time investment given the constraints
  • ✘ Inability to add multiple emails for group tickets
  • ✘ Lack of control over ticket sales
  • ✘ Inconsistent use of seating charts and diagrams

As a result, for attendees, we needed to make sure to implement a simple, streamlined, and aesthetically pleasing concept. For the managers of the site, we needed to reduce the amount of time they take to manage the site. To do so, we planned to integrate our ticketing site directly into their website, eliminate the problem with the group tickets, and set up an online seating chart.


❂ Design: Wireframing

We used four criteria to decide which concept we would implement in our solution. Affordability was important given that our client is a non-profit and has limited funds to carry out a large event. Usability was another important criteria since we wanted the event-goers to be able to quickly and efficiently purchase their tickets, leading us to make the software as intuitive as possible. Social impact was another one due to the importance of spreading awareness about the Rotary Club and its service activities. Feasibility was the last criteria because of the limited time we were given and the necessity of getting the solution implemented in time for next year's event. With these four criteria in mind, we created two prototypes from existing widgets such as Eventbrite and Universe as well as our own Mock-Up wireframe.

3rd Phase

❀ Develop: Iteration & Concept Selection

After testing 3 different prototypes to users, we found that the integrated widget was the most balanced concept and had them most to offer our client. We leaned more towards the Eventbrite widget, since it has a secure, flexible, and large support service and our client expressed his approval for it. Another advantage of choosing the widget as our selected concept was that we can incorporate elements of the ground-up HTML/CSS website into the solution while taking advantage of a trusted service. Though creating it from ground-up would be ideal as it would come as no cost to both our client and the ticket buyers, we concluded that it would not be very feasible due to time and other constraints. After we receieved access to the code for the widget once we selected our layout, we redesigned the user interface of the client’s website to incorporate all required information with brand consistency and the widget at the heart of the page. The webpage also contains an interactive map, showing the location of the concert and the Facebook Page for the event.

❐ Evaluate: Usability Testing

Now that our prototype is in its final stage, we decided to test it out with our client John and the same people we initially interviewed. We had them go through the process of locating the event and purchasing a combination of lawn, table seats, and VIP table tickets. For testing purposes, we made all the tickets free of cost. Due to the request of our client, we decided to not let our buyers select their own seating arrangements, so we were unable to show the user where they will be sitting during the actual event. Thus, we planned to create a seating chart for the venue that corresponds to the seating assignment on each ticket to remedy this problem.

    Purchase Procedure
  1. User selects the desired ticket type and quantity on the widget and clicks the “Order Now” button.
  2. User is redirected to Eventbrite checkout page where they can view the total cost and enter billing and ticket holder information.
  3. Once the order is placed, the user receives the tickets immediately by email and can either print out tickets or use the Eventbrite app to display their tickets at the venue.


Even though there was minimal testing done on mobile, we went through the process ourselves to work on resolving issues such as difficulty scrolling through ticket options without accidentally scrolling down the website. Since mobile devices are a convenient way for users to show their tickets at the door, we recommended users and the client to utlilize the Eventbrite app to pull up their tickets (QR code) on the phone to scan. Besides the minor issues with the mobile aspect, the main concern that emerged from testing is with the VIP tables. Many users including our client, found it confusing that the only choices for this option were 0 OR 10 tickets. We intended to define 10 as 10 seats at the table, but one of the users mentioned that it would make more sense to count it as 1 ticket for 1 table.

Through many testing and iterations, we found out not only is the VIP table option confusing, but also that during checkout, each ticket asks for a name, email, and mailing address. Users had to tediously fill out this information 10 times under eight minutes, so we kept these concerns in mind as we further refine our prototype.

Conclusion

❈ Aftermath

To address the concerns about the VIP Table option that arose from user testing, there is now a “More Info” button next to each of the ticket types, providing a brief description about the differences in ticket seating. We decided to leave 0 or 10 option as-is because we want each attendee to have a ticket for easy access into the event. Additionally, we removed the extra, time-consuming process of inputting user's home address when purchasing group tickets. Now, there is the option of copying the data from another ticket and the email address of one person.

With our design finalized, our client may choose to either incorporate the widget into their own website, or take inspiration from our design and move onto a different ticketing service. If our client chooses to use our design, he can view our product manual to make any necessary changes for future events, such as event information and seating arrangement map. Overall, our solution appropriately completes our client’s requirements while still being feasible and affordable. Accordingly, our research consisted of personally finding and testing ticketing services and conducting user testing on selected ticketing services. This research and development paved the way for our final design, a fully functional web page with a built-in eventbrite widget.

☾ Final Thoughts

I had the honor of working with 6 other wonderful goal-driven individuals as my first design-based team project. We all carried out our responsibilities as different roles, and needless to say, it was one of the best group projects I've experienced. Working with a real-world client, John Morley, along with organization's board members with such compassionate hearts was definitely inspiring. This is where I opened my eyes to creating not only user-friendly but also all-inclusive designs, by seeing how the Rotary Club selflessly gives back to the community. As a Client Liaison, I learned how to effectively communicate with the client and help team members understand his needs or expectations. From holding user interviews and designing necessary materials (e.g. logo, manual) to visiting the event site, this project was an unforgettable experience that I'll forever cherish.
