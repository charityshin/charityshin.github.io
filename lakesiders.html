CONCERT AT THE LAKES

Project Details


VIEW PROTOTYPE

Overview

Context

  • Course: Design for Development (ENG 100D)
  • Duration: 10 weeks | 2017
  • Team: 6 members | cross-functional
  • Client: Santee Lakeside Rotary Club

To tackle the issue of r, we worked with a nonprofit organization that gives back to the community in various ways, such as holding an annual event known as Concert at the Lakes. The goal of this event is to raise the necessary funds to provide incentives for high school students to graduate, and this incentive is a two-year scholarship to a college or to a business or trade school of their choice.


Problem
How to create a better ticketing system for an annual nonprofit organization event?

Concert at the Lakes is such a popular event amongst Santee residents that tickets are sold out every year, so having a good ticketing system was essential. The previous event site redirected users to TicketRiver, where they can purchase a ticket for either a seat at a reserved table or an unreserved seating in an open lawn area. To add on, if users wanted to purchase a whole Table (for a group of people), they would have to go back to the original site and scroll down to fill out a different form. Thus, managers of Concert at the Lakes needed a better ticketing system because of current complications with seating arrangements and group ticket distributions. Our goal was to provide better user experience for ticket-buyers and better management system for site managers from Rotary Club.

1st Phase

The first phase consisted of understanding our audience and stakeholders. We conducted user interviews to gain insight on how potential concert-goers navigate through ticketing websites, such as which ticketing system are they most familiar with. Through this, we were able to learn more about the users and their areas of needs. For attendees, we needed to make sure to implement a simple, streamlined, and aesthetically pleasing concept. For the managers of the site, we needed to reduce the amount of time they take to manage the site. To do so, we planned to integrate our ticketing site directly into their website, eliminate the problem with the group tickets, and set up an online seating chart.


  

2nd Phase

We used four criteria to decide which concept we would implement in our solution. Affordability was important given that our client is a non-profit and has limited funds to carry out a large event. Usability was another important criteria since we wanted the event-goers to be able to quickly and efficiently purchase their tickets, leading us to make the software as intuitive as possible. Social impact was another one due to the importance of spreading awareness about the Rotary Club and its service activities. Feasibility was the last criteria because of the limited time we were given and the necessity of getting the solution implemented in time for next year's event. With these four criteria in mind, we created two prototypes from existing widgets such as Eventbrite and Universe as well as our own Mock-Up prototype.

3rd Phase

In this phase, we found that the integrated widget was the most balanced concept and had them most to offer our client. We leaned more towards the Eventbrite widget, since it has a large support service and our client expressed his approval for it. Another advantage of choosing the widget as our selected concept was that we can incorporate elements of the ground-up HTML/CSS website into the solution while taking advantage of a trusted service. After we receieved access to the code for the widget once we selected our layout, we redesigned the User Interface of the client’s website to incorporate all required information and the widget at the heart of the page. The webpage also contains an interactive map, showing the location of the concert and the Facebook Page for the event.



Aftermath

We focused our efforts on finding the most user-friendly and dynamic ticketing widget available. Accordingly, our research consisted of personally finding and testing ticketing services and conducting user testing on selected ticketing services. This research and development paved the way for our final design, a fully functional web page with a built-in eventbrite widget. And with our finalized, our client can incorporate the widget into their own website and can view our product manual to learn how to make any necessary changes for future events, such as event information and correct seating arrangement map.
