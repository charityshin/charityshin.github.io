Poki Yaki Gift Certificate

Project Details

OVERVIEW

CONTEXT

An individual project completed during the summer of 2017 for a restaruant, Poki Yaki, in which I have been responsible for branding and promoting since 2015.


ROLE

I was mainly in charge of designing the gift certificate after the manager of the restaurant gave me the necessary information and preferences. I came up with ideas, and then received approval after several phases of discussing as a group and re-thinking and editing independently. It was also important to pay special attention to details, especially when submitting the finished product to a card-printing company.



PROBLEM

After creating restaurant’s logo, stamp card, and business card, I was asked to come up with a gift certificate design. As the business brand has a distinctive color and theme, it was important to incorporate this into the gift certificate as well. However, throughout the process, we came to a realization that it's important to keep gift certificates fairly conventional.


Process

First Phase

The first phase consisted of looking at old designs made for the business, and seeing what I can incorporate into the gift certificate design. The left side shows a design of stamp card, which was the first card design I created back in 2015. As time passed, we realized the importance of having an eye-catching and attention-grabbing brand color that stands out even from far away, so I made a switch from light orange to a brighter orange (shown in business card on the right side). From this, some of the features that I knew I had to include in the certificate design were the logo and brief business contact information.


  

SECOND PHASE

In the beginning, the manager and I considered to have the certificate to be a longer length, around the size of dollar bills. Even though most gift certificates are like that, we came to realization that having it a smaller size (size of average business cards) will be easier and more convenient for people to put inside the card slots of their wallets, rather than having a bill-sized card-stock paper. So, I started to design in a standard business card dimension, 3.5 x 2, and gained insight from the stamp card design. You can now see in these two prototypes that the color is changed to a brighter orange, which can be both a marketing and a user-centered design approach.


  

THIRD PHASE

This is when I realized that a gift certificate should look like a gift certificate, as Steven Krug mentioned— stay conventional! Because we already made a decision to not have a conventional dimension, something about this certificate had to look more like a certificate. From there, I played around with fonts and placings of texts and borders, and from there, the gift certificate started to look more legitimate.




FINAL PHASE

The outcome of this project was definitely different from what I first had in mind. The orange background still gave the feeling of a business card rather than a gift certificate, so a new background was added in. This matches with what the restaurant serves, and the color theme is still shown to be consistent with the orange border, logo, and font. There were many tweaks and edits made throughout the process, but the outcome made it all worthwhile. The design was carefully reviewed by the manager and owner, who approved of this final design. The final step was to make the card orders, and in the end, the clients received actual physical cards of the design.
